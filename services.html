Common Questions

Wedding Officiant FAQ

Clear answers help couples plan with confidence and avoid last-minute confusion.

Do we need to get our own marriage license?

Yes. The couple is responsible for obtaining a valid New York State marriage license before the ceremony. The officiant cannot legally perform the ceremony unless the license is present and valid on the wedding day.

Do you perform civil ceremonies?

Yes. Next Steps Wedding Solutions offers respectful civil ceremonies for couples who want a professional, nonreligious ceremony that is still meaningful and legally sound.

Do you perform faith-friendly or Christian ceremonies?

Yes. As an ordained chaplain, ceremonies may include prayer, Scripture, blessing, covenant language, or other faith elements when desired by the couple.

Can we write our own vows?

Yes. Couples may write their own vows, use traditional vows, or receive guidance in shaping vows that are clear, sincere, and appropriate for the ceremony.

Do you attend wedding rehearsals?

Rehearsal attendance is included with the Premium Custom Ceremony package and may be added to other packages when available.

How do we reserve our ceremony date?

A date is reserved only after availability is confirmed, the service agreement is completed, and the required retainer is received. Submitting an inquiry does not reserve the date.

Do you travel outside Monroe County?

Yes. Next Steps Wedding Solutions serves Rochester, Monroe County, Western New York, the Finger Lakes, and surrounding communities. Travel fees may apply depending on location.

What happens after the ceremony?

After the ceremony, the marriage license must be completed and returned according to the issuing clerk's instructions. Next Steps Wedding Solutions will complete the officiant portion as part of the ceremony process.
