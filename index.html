Do You Know How the New York Marriage License Works?

Before a wedding ceremony can be legally performed in New York, the couple must obtain a valid marriage license from a town or city clerk. The officiant does not issue the license, but the license must be present on the wedding day.

We created a simple guide to help couples understand the basic timing, responsibilities, and reminders before the ceremony.

Read the Marriage License Guide

Quick License Reminders

  • The couple must obtain the marriage license.
  • The license must be valid on the ceremony date.
  • The license must be physically present at the ceremony.
  • The officiant completes the ceremony/license portion after the vows.
  • Requirements should be confirmed with the issuing town or city clerk.
